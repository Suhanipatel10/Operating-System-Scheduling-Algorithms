Round Robin theory

Round robin scheduling is a pre-emptive process scheduling algorithm that allocates a fixed time slice to each process in a cyclic manner.It ensures fairness by allowing each process to execute for a specific time period, typically called a time quantum or time slice, before moving to the next process in the queue.If a process doesn't finish within its time slice, it's preempted and placed at the end of the queue to wait for its next turn. This algorithm is commonly used in time-sharing environments where multiple processes compete for CPU time, ensuring that no process monopolizes the CPU and all processes get a fair share of execution time.
